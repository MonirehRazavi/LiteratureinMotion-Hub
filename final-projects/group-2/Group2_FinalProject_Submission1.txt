Group 2 - From Orality to Archive 

Subject: Indigenous and oral literatures enter global circulation through colonial translation and archiving

Topic: Analyzing how colonial translations of Indigenous literatures, specifically, Inuit, Ojibwe, Cree, and First Nations, enabled their entry into global circulation. 

Goal: To investigate how Indigenous groups such as the Inuit, Ojibwe, Cree, and First Nations have circulated beyond their local contexts, through the process of translation and archiving. 

Using Properties:
SELECT ?work ?workLabel ?author ?authorLabel ?ethnicityLabel WHERE {
  ?work wdt:P31 wd:Q7725634;  # instance of literary work
        wdt:P50 ?author.               # author
  ?author wdt:P172 ?ethnicity.         # ethnicity of the author
 
  VALUES ?ethnicity {
    wd:Q189975   # Inuit
    wd:Q255872   # Ojibwe
    wd:Q117191   # Cree
    wd:Q262457   # First Nations
  }

}
