Group 1 — Storytelling, Identity, and Satire in Thomas King’s Works

Subject:
Contemporary Indigenous literature and narrative sovereignty in Canada.

Topic:
An exploration of Thomas King’s works — focusing on how his writing blends oral storytelling traditions, satire, and political critique to reframe Indigenous identity in Canadian literature.

Goal:
To analyze the transnational presence and cultural impact of Thomas King’s literature by examining translations, adaptations, and publication networks recorded in Wikidata. The project seeks to understand how King’s works circulate globally and how his storytelling strategies challenge colonial narratives.

SPARQL Query:

SELECT DISTINCT ?author ?authorLabel ?work ?workLabel ?language ?languageLabel ?publisher ?publisherLabel WHERE {
  ?author wdt:P31 wd:Q5;              # instance of human
          wdt:P106 wd:Q482980;        # occupation: author
          wdt:P27 wd:Q16;             # country of citizenship: Canada
          wdt:P214 ?viafID.           # VIAF identifier for cross-checking authority data
  FILTER(?author = wd:Q779698)        # Thomas King (Wikidata ID)

  ?work wdt:P50 ?author;              # author of the work
        wdt:P407 ?language.           # language of the work
  OPTIONAL { ?work wdt:P123 ?publisher. }  # publisher (if available)

  SERVICE wikibase:label { bd:serviceParam wikibase:language "en". }
}
